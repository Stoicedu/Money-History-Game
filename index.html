[Second page] Currency History Timeline - Correct Answers

The Correct Timeline of Money Evolution

From ancient bartering to digital currencies

9000-6000 BC

1. Barter System

The earliest form of exchange where goods and services were directly traded without using money. Communities exchanged what they had surplus of for what they needed.

1200 BC

2. Cowrie Shells

The first use of cowrie shells as a standardized currency began in China. These small, durable shells were widely accepted across Asia and Africa for centuries.

640 BC

3. Chinese Spade Coins

The world's oldest known coin minting site in China produced bronze spade coins. These tool-shaped coins were the first standardized metal currency.

600 BC

4. Lydian Coins

King Alyattes of Lydia (now Turkey) minted what is considered the first official currency using electrum, a natural alloy of gold and silver.

806 AD

5. Paper Money

The first known paper banknotes appeared in China during the Tang Dynasty. These "flying money" notes replaced the heavy strings of metal coins for large transactions.

1816 AD

6. Gold Standard

England officially made gold the standard of value, linking currency directly to gold. This created a fixed exchange rate system that would spread worldwide.

1930s

7. End of Gold Standard

The Great Depression led countries to abandon the gold standard, starting a new era of fiat money where currency value was determined by governments rather than gold.

2009 AD

8. Bitcoin

The first decentralized digital cryptocurrency was introduced by Satoshi Nakamoto, marking the beginning of blockchain-based currencies not controlled by any central authority.

Fun fact: While monetary systems have evolved dramatically over 11,000 years, the basic concept remains the same: creating a trusted medium to facilitate trade and store value.
